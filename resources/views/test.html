1. Cell Theory

All living things are made up of cells, cells are the basic unit of structure and function in living things, and all cells arise from pre-existing cells.

2. Evolution

Changes in species over time through natural selection and genetic drift. Organisms that are better adapted to their environment tend to survive and produce more offspring.

3. Genetics

DNA carries hereditary information, genes control traits, and genetic information is passed from parents to offspring through reproduction.

4. Homeostasis

Living organisms maintain internal stability through various feedback mechanisms to survive in changing environments.

5. Energy Flow

Energy flows through ecosystems, starting with photosynthesis in plants and moving through food chains and food webs.

6. Structure and Function

The structure of biological systems is directly related to their function, from molecular to ecosystem levels.

7. Information Flow

Biological systems communicate and regulate through signal transmission, from nerve impulses to hormonal signals.

8. Reproduction

All living things reproduce, either sexually or asexually, passing genetic information to the next generation.

9. Growth and Development

Organisms grow and develop following specific patterns controlled by genes and environmental factors.

10. Interdependence

Living things depend on each other and their environment. No organism exists in isolation.
